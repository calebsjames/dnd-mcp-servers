# D&D 5e Terminology

Core terms used throughout the dnd-ui application.

## Ability Score
One of the six core attributes: Strength (STR), Dexterity (DEX), Constitution (CON), Intelligence (INT), Wisdom (WIS), Charisma (CHA). Each ranges from 1–20 for typical characters (up to 30 with magic). The ability modifier is (score - 10) / 2, rounded down.

## Ability Modifier
Derived from an ability score: (score - 10) / 2, rounded down. A score of 10–11 gives +0, 12–13 gives +1, 14–15 gives +2, 8–9 gives -1, etc.

## Advantage
Roll two d20s and take the higher result. Granted by spells, class features, or favorable conditions.

## Disadvantage
Roll two d20s and take the lower result. Applied by spells, conditions, or unfavorable circumstances.

## Armor Class
**Armor Class (AC)**: The number an attacker must meet or beat with their attack roll to hit. Base AC = 10 + DEX modifier (no armor). Wearing armor changes the formula depending on armor type.

## Attack Roll
1d20 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus (if proficient). Must meet or exceed the target's AC to hit.

## Cantrip
A 0-level spell that can be cast at will without expending spell slots. Does not scale with spell slots.

## Challenge Rating
**Challenge Rating (CR)**: A measure of a monster's power. CR equals the level at which four characters can expect a medium difficulty encounter. Values: 0, 1/8, 1/4, 1/2, 1–30.

## Concentration
A condition on certain spells. A character can only concentrate on one spell at a time. Concentration is broken by taking damage (DC 10 or half damage, whichever is higher, Constitution saving throw), being incapacitated, casting another concentration spell, or dying.

## Condition
A status effect applied to a creature. Common conditions: Blinded, Charmed, Deafened, Exhaustion, Frightened, Grappled, Incapacitated, Invisible, Paralyzed, Petrified, Poisoned, Prone, Restrained, Stunned, Unconscious.

## Critical Hit
Rolling a 20 on the d20 for an attack roll (a natural 20). Doubles the number of damage dice rolled.

## Damage Roll
Dice roll to determine damage dealt. Damage type matters for resistances and immunities (slashing, piercing, bludgeoning, fire, cold, lightning, acid, poison, psychic, radiant, necrotic, thunder, force).

## Death Saving Throw
When a character drops to 0 HP, they make death saving throws on each of their turns. Three successes stabilizes them; three failures kills them. Rolling a 1 counts as two failures; rolling a 20 regains 1 HP.

## Exhaustion
A condition with 6 levels of increasing severity. Level 1: disadvantage on ability checks. Level 2: speed halved. Level 3: disadvantage on attack rolls and saving throws. Level 4: HP maximum halved. Level 5: speed reduced to 0. Level 6: death.

## Hit Dice
Class-specific dice used to determine HP gained per level. Rolled during short rests to recover HP. Also determines HP at 1st level (max value + CON modifier).

## Hit Points
**HP**: A measure of a creature's health. Reduced by damage, restored by healing. Dropping to 0 causes incapacitation/death saving throws for characters. Full HP restored by long rest.

## Initiative
Determines turn order in combat. Rolled at the start of each combat: 1d20 + DEX modifier. Higher goes first.

## Long Rest
8 hours of rest (6 hours sleep for non-elves). Restores all HP, all spell slots, and most class feature uses.

## Opportunity Attack
A melee attack made as a reaction when a hostile creature leaves your reach. Does not require an action.

## Passive Perception
10 + Perception modifier. Used to notice things without actively searching.

## Proficiency Bonus
A bonus added to rolls with proficient skills, saving throws, attack rolls, or tools. Scales with character level: +2 (L1-4), +3 (L5-8), +4 (L9-12), +5 (L13-16), +6 (L17-20).

## Reaction
A special action taken outside your turn, typically once per round. Examples: opportunity attack, shield spell, counterspell.

## Saving Throw
A defensive roll to resist an effect. 1d20 + ability modifier + proficiency bonus (if proficient in that save). Must meet or beat the spell/effect's DC.

## Short Rest
1 hour of rest. Allows spending Hit Dice to recover HP. Some class features (e.g., Warlock spell slots, Fighter Action Surge) restore on short rest.

## Skill
A specific application of an ability score. Each ability has associated skills. Proficiency adds the proficiency bonus to rolls.

## Spell Attack Roll
An attack roll made for spells requiring attack rolls. 1d20 + spellcasting ability modifier + proficiency bonus.

## Spell Save DC
The DC enemies must beat to resist your spells. DC = 8 + proficiency bonus + spellcasting ability modifier.

## Spell Slot
A resource expended to cast a spell. Recovered on long rest (except Warlock, which recovers on short rest). Slots are organized by level (1–9); a higher-level slot can be used to cast a lower-level spell.

## Upcasting
Casting a lower-level spell using a higher-level spell slot for increased effect. Only applies to spells that specify upcast effects.
